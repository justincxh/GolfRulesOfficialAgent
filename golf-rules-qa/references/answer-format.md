# Answer Format

## Fact Checklist

Before answering, identify whether any missing fact could change the ruling:

- Competition format: stroke play, match play, four-ball, foursomes, casual.
- Area of the course: teeing area, general area, bunker, penalty area, putting
  green, out of bounds.
- Ball state: in motion, at rest, lifted, moved, marked, replaced, dropped,
  substituted, lost.
- Actor/cause: player, caddie, opponent, partner, outside influence, natural
  forces.
- Intent: accidental, deliberate, authorized, unknown.
- Local Rules or Committee conditions.

Ask only for facts that materially affect the ruling.

## Recommended Response

Use this order:

1. Ruling: short conclusion.
2. Procedure: exact next action.
3. Penalty: none, one penalty stroke, general penalty, loss of hole, or DQ.
4. Rule basis: rule number, definition, clarification, or Model Local Rule.
5. Caveats: facts that would change the answer.
6. Confidence and source path: local Markdown corpus or online official source.

## Examples

Question: In stroke play, my ball accidentally hits my own bag and comes to rest
in the fairway. Is there a penalty?

Answer shape: No penalty. Play the ball as it lies from the fairway. Rule 11.1a
has no penalty when a ball in motion accidentally hits any person or outside
influence, including a player's equipment; Rule 11.1b normally requires the ball
to be played as it lies when played from off the putting green.

Question: In match play, my opponent putts on the green and accidentally hits my
unmarked ball at rest on the green.

Answer shape: Usually no penalty to the opponent under Rule 11.1a. The
opponent's ball is played as it lies unless another putting-green replay rule
applies; the moved ball at rest is replaced under Rule 9.6 / Rule 11 context
depending on the facts. Confirm whether both balls were on the putting green
before the stroke and whether this is match play or stroke play.
